Projects

  • Visualizing Bentley's University water usage

    This project focused on visualizing Bentley University's inconsistent water usage data to help the Office of Sustainability. The solution was an accessible dashboard, "Plastic Bottles Avoided," featuring a colorblind-friendly palette and clear layout. It helps staff quickly identify trends, track progress, and locate problematic water stations.

    • accessibility
    • data visualization

  • Tomato Dice

    The "Tomato Dice" is a multimodal device designed to help remote workers take effective breaks through timeboxing, using visual and audio cues. After a year of ideation and prototyping, a usability study found users were fascinated by the dice and more likely to use it for relaxation. Recommendations included customization, volume control, and a silent mode for shared workspaces.

    • prototyping
    • functional analysis
    • sentiment and thematic analysis
    • user interviews

  • Number Pools: Simplifying Call Tracking Management

    A SaaS platform needed to simplify its complex call tracking system, which frustrated users. Through mixed-methods research, we redesigned the number pool management interface and setup wizard. This resulted in a 30% reduction in support tickets and 95% positive beta feedback, streamlining the call tracking experience.

    • quantitative research

  • Mobile rhythm game prototype

    This project involved prototyping a mobile rhythm game for iPhones, allowing users to play their own music by tapping a grid. The design process relied heavily on literature research to inform gameplay and engagement elements, with prototypes created in UXPin and Keynote. Future steps include formal usability testing and developing an algorithm for beat map creation.

  • Lifestyle products usability research

    An academic team conducted a four-month mixed-methods study for an undisclosed robotics company, exploring factors influencing the adoption of "lifestyle products." Through interviews and a diary study with 20 participants, we identified key usage facilitators and inhibitors. The findings and recommendations were delivered to the client's UX team for product reevaluation.
